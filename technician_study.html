Self Study Course

Getting Your USA Amateur Radio Technician License

This course is designed as a simple self paced guide to help anyone prepare for the FCC Technician Class license exam. It focuses on the essentials: plain language explanations of key concepts, the technical knowledge required to pass the test, and links to official resources like the FCC question pool.

No prior experience is required. Simply work through the modules, review the topics, and practice using the tools provided.

The course follows the ten Technician subelements (T0 through T9) used in the FCC question pool. Each module includes an overview, key topics, simple technical explanations, and links to additional study resources.

Getting Started

  • What is Amateur Radio? Amateur radio is a hobby and public service where licensed operators use radio frequencies for communication, experimentation, and emergency support.
  • Technician License is the entry level license. The exam contains 35 multiple choice questions and you must answer 26 correctly to pass.

Module 1 – FCC Rules (T1)

  • Purpose of amateur radio service
  • License classes: Technician, General, Extra
  • Station identification every 10 minutes
  • Prohibited transmissions such as business or music

Technicians primarily operate on VHF and UHF bands including 2 meters (144–148 MHz) and 70 centimeters (420–450 MHz).
