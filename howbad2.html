The Next Step

O.K., so you have your bug out pack, some weapons, your car, scanner and shortwave and a will to survive. Good. You're also scared to death. GOOD! You should be. You know where you want to go. The question is, can you get there in one piece? Most likely, everyone and his brother will have the same idea as you except, you are part of a small percentage that is at least somewhat if not completely prepared. Most of the sheep, when surprised and stampeding will have no idea of what they are doing. You are faced with a profound question; How ruthless do I have to be to survive? The answer is very. A reality of the situation may be that you might just have to kill someone or even many if they get in your way. Remember, your 'Prime Directive' at this point is protect yourself, your family or if you are in a survival group, your compatriots and get to where you need to go. The Police will NOT be your allies. They will be overtaxed dealing with the out of control criminal element and the sheep stampede. If you get caught in the middle, the Police will probably think you're a threat and cut you down all the same. Would I shoot a cop or an authority figure? Probably. Especially if he were trying to keep me from defending myself or keep me from getting to where I need to go. If he crosses that line, he has become no ally of mine. Just look at the civil unrest in some of the third-world countries to get preview of what might be.

If you don't have a safe haven anywhere out of the hot zone, you need to familiarize yourself with any heavily wooded areas away from the hot zone. National Parks and Bureau of Land Management areas are good. In other words, someplace you feel may be reasonably safe that you can "crash" at and give yourself some time to assess the situation. You may want to stay there if it is a really safe place or you may want to move on later to find a safer place. This area may also be handy as an interim hideout until you can get to your safe place. That may also depend on how bad things were when you left. If you research this before things get messy, it may make the difference. Make sure your safe haven has good cover, a water source of some kind and a good escape route, just in case. Rocky and hilly terrain is even better in my opinion, because it's easier to defend and blend into.
