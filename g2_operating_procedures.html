Operating Procedures (G2)

Overview & Why Study This

HF operation introduces new procedures for DX, nets, CW, and digital modes. G2 expands on Technician T2 operating practices with HF-specific conventions, breaking into pile-ups, and emergency protocols. It connects directly to your Technician voice and repeater skills while adding the international “Q” code usage and band-plan etiquette required for General HF privileges.

Key Topics

  • Phone operating procedures (USB/LSB conventions, DX pile-ups)
  • CW procedures and Q signals
  • Digital mode operating (FT8, RTTY, PSK31)
  • Band plans and emergency communications (RACES/ARES)
  • HF net operation and traffic handling

Official Resources & Pools

  • Current General Pool: https://hamstudy.org/general

Sample Questions from the General Question Pool

G2A01

Which mode is normally used for HF phone operation on the 80-meter band?

A. USB

B. LSB

C. AM

D. FM

Correct answer: B

G2B01

What is the purpose of the “Q” signal “QSL”?

A. To confirm receipt of a message

B. To request a signal report

C. To end a contact

D. To ask for location

Correct answer: A

G2E01

What is the most common method of sending and receiving digital signals on HF?

A. Packet

B. FT8

C. RTTY

D. All of these choices are correct

Correct answer: D

Answer Key

  1. B
  2. A
  3. D
