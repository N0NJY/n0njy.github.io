Module 6: Circuit Components

Subelement G6 — Semiconductors, Batteries, Ferrites, and Logic Gates

By Rick Donaldson, NØNJY — n0njy@qsl.net

6.1 Introduction

Amateur radio equipment is built from a variety of electronic components. General Class questions cover semiconductors, batteries, ferrite cores, and digital logic. Understanding these components helps you troubleshoot, repair, and modify equipment.

6.2 Semiconductor Devices

Diodes: Allow current in one direction only. Forward voltage drop: ~0.6–0.7V silicon, ~0.3V germanium, 1.8–3.5V LEDs. PIV (Peak Inverse Voltage) is the maximum reverse voltage a diode can withstand without breakdown.

Zener Diode: Designed to operate in reverse breakdown at a specific voltage. Used for voltage regulation.

BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor): Current-controlled device. Small base current controls larger collector current. Used as amplifiers and switches.

FET (Field-Effect Transistor): Voltage-controlled device. Gate voltage controls drain current. Very high input impedance. Common in RF applications.

6.3 Ferrite Cores

Ferrite is a ceramic magnetic material used in inductors, transformers, and chokes. Toroidal cores confine the magnetic field within the core, reducing interaction with nearby components. Different ferrite mix numbers (Type 31, 43, 61) are optimized for different frequency ranges. Ferrite beads slipped over a cable attenuate RF at high frequencies, preventing interference.

6.4 Batteries

Lead-acid batteries: Fully charged at 12.6V. Minimum discharge voltage for maximum life: 10.5 volts. Discharging below this permanently damages the plates.

Lithium Iron Phosphate (LiFePO4): Lighter, higher energy density, tolerates deeper discharge than lead-acid. Requires a battery management system (BMS) and a lithium-compatible charger.

6.5 Digital Logic

Basic gates: AND (high only when all inputs high), OR (high when any input high), NOT (inverts input), NAND/NOR (AND/OR with inverted outputs), XOR (high when inputs differ). Truth tables describe the output for every combination of inputs.

Module 6 Practice Questions

Q1 (G6A01) — What is the minimum allowable discharge voltage for maximum life of a standard 12-volt lead-acid battery?

  1. 10.5 volts
  2. 11.5 volts
  3. 12.0 volts
  4. 9.0 volts

Q2 (G6B01) — What is the peak inverse voltage rating of a rectifier?

  1. The maximum forward voltage it can handle before breaking down
  2. The maximum reverse voltage it can handle without breaking down
  3. The minimum forward current required for the device to conduct
  4. The reverse current that flows when forward biased

Q3 (G6B05) — What is a common use for a ferrite bead?

  1. As a high-power resonant circuit element
  2. To reduce or eliminate RF interference on cables
  3. To increase the efficiency of a power amplifier
  4. To block DC current in an RF circuit

Q4 (G6A09) — What is an advantage of the low internal resistance of nickel-cadmium batteries?

  1. Long life under trickle charge conditions
  2. High energy density
  3. They can deliver high current pulses without significant voltage drop
  4. They are non-toxic and environmentally safe

Q5 (G6B03) — Which is the output of a two-input NOR gate when both inputs are logic “0”?

  1. Logic “0”
  2. Logic “1”
  3. The inputs are added
  4. Undefined

Q6 (G6A11) — Which of the following is an important characteristic of a toroidal core inductor?

  1. Large coupling to nearby components
  2. Requires a large air gap
  3. Self-shielding due to the magnetic field confined within the core
  4. High radiation of magnetic fields

Q7 (G6B07) — Which of the following describes a field-effect transistor?

  1. A current-controlled device where base current controls collector current
  2. A voltage-controlled device where gate voltage controls drain current
  3. A device that only operates in a saturated state
  4. A device requiring both forward and reverse biasing

Q8 (G6A03) — What is the approximate junction threshold voltage of a germanium diode?

  1. 0.7 volts
  2. 0.1 volts
  3. 0.3 volts
  4. 1.0 volts

Answer Key

  1. A — 10.5 volts is the minimum discharge voltage for lead-acid battery longevity
  2. B — PIV is the maximum reverse voltage a rectifier can withstand
  3. B — Ferrite beads reduce RF interference on cables
  4. C — Low internal resistance allows high current delivery without voltage drop
  5. B — NOR output is logic “1” only when both inputs are “0”
  6. C — Toroidal cores self-shield by confining the magnetic field
  7. B — FETs are voltage-controlled devices
  8. C — Germanium diode forward voltage drop is approximately 0.3 volts
