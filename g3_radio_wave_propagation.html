Radio Wave Propagation (G3)

Overview & Why Study This

HF propagation is completely different from the VHF/UHF line-of-sight you learned in Technician T3. G3 teaches skywave, ionospheric effects, and solar influences so you can predict when and where your HF signals will reach. This module directly builds on T3 by adding long-distance HF concepts that determine whether you can work Europe from North Carolina on 20 meters.

Key Topics

  • Sunspots, solar flux, and geomagnetic activity
  • Maximum Usable Frequency (MUF) and Lowest Usable Frequency (LUF)
  • Ionospheric layers and refraction
  • Short-path vs. long-path propagation
  • NVIS (Near Vertical Incidence Skywave) for regional coverage
  • Scatter modes and gray-line propagation

G3A01

A. It affects ionospheric propagation

B. It has no effect on radio propagation

C. It only affects VHF

D. It controls aurora

Correct answer: A

What is the Maximum Usable Frequency (MUF)?

B. The lowest frequency for skywave

C. The frequency that causes maximum absorption

D. The frequency for ground-wave only

Correct answer: A
