Module 8: Signals and Emissions

Subelement G8 — Modulation, Digital Modes, Bandwidth, and Spurious Emissions

By Rick Donaldson, NØNJY — n0njy@qsl.net

8.1 Emission Type Designators

The FCC standardized emission designators:

  • A1A: CW (keyed carrier)
  • A3E: AM double-sideband full carrier
  • J3E: SSB suppressed carrier (what we call “SSB”)
  • F3E: FM analog voice
  • F1B: FSK (RTTY)

8.2 Modulation Modes

AM: Carrier amplitude varies with audio. Requires twice the bandwidth of SSB. Carrier present even when no audio.

SSB: Carrier and one sideband removed. All power in the information-bearing sideband. Bandwidth approximately half that of AM.

FM: Carrier frequency varies with audio amplitude. Constant amplitude. Excellent noise rejection. Standard for VHF/UHF voice.

8.3 Digital Modes

RTTY: FSK at 45.45 baud, 170 Hz shift. ~250 Hz occupied bandwidth.

PSK31: Phase-shift keying at 31.25 baud. ~31 Hz bandwidth. Excellent for marginal conditions.

FT8: 8-FSK, 15-second cycles, 50 Hz bandwidth. Can decode 20 dB below noise floor. Dominant weak-signal mode.

Packet/APRS: AX.25 protocol. 1200 baud on VHF. Integrates with GPS for position tracking.

8.5 Spurious Emissions

Harmonics: Integer multiples of the transmit frequency. A 14 MHz transmitter may produce harmonics at 28, 42 MHz, etc. Suppressed by a low-pass filter at the transmitter output.

Intermodulation distortion (IMD): Products at various frequency combinations when signals mix in a non-linear device. Overdriven SSB transmitters create splatter — unwanted sidebands across the band.

Parasitic oscillations: Unintended RF oscillation caused by stray capacitance and inductance in amplifier stages. Prevented by parasitic suppressors (small resistors or ferrite beads).

Answer Key

  1. B — FSK shifts the transmitted frequency between two discrete values
  2. B — Symbol rate is the rate at which the waveform changes to convey information
  3. C — FT8 uses structured protocol to enable contacts in noise
  4. C — Frequency modulation varies only the frequency of the carrier
  5. C — VHF FM repeater voice is approximately 10–15 kHz wide
  6. B — APRS integrates packet radio and GPS for position tracking
  7. A — CDMA allows multiple users to share the same channel simultaneously
  8. C — Unwanted signals outside the transmitted bandwidth are spurious emissions
