General Class Amateur Radio License

Self-Study Course for Technician Operators Upgrading to General

By Rick Donaldson, NØNJY — n0njy@qsl.net

This course is designed for licensed Technician Class operators preparing to upgrade to General Class. The General Class license opens the HF bands — the worldwide bands — giving you access to 160 through 10 meters with up to 1,500 watts PEP output. If you have been limited to VHF and UHF since getting your Technician ticket, this upgrade changes everything.

Each module covers one major subelement of the FCC General Class examination. Read the lesson content thoroughly, then work through the practice questions at the end of each module. The exam questions are drawn directly from the official FCC question pool (valid July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2027).

Pool Note: This course is based on the FCC General Class question pool effective July 1, 2023 through June 30, 2027. The General Class pool is not changing until 2027. Study confidently from this material.

Study Tips

Work through the modules in order. Later material builds on earlier concepts. After completing all modules, use the practice exam tools below to simulate the actual test. The General Class exam consists of 35 questions drawn from the official pool; you need 26 correct (74%) to pass.

  • HamStudy.org — Free online practice exams that track your weak areas
  • QRZ.com Ham Radio Exams — Full simulated exams from the actual pool
  • ARRL.org — General Class License Manual and study resources
