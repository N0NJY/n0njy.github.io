June 1999 - Y2K Report

 

On or about March 5th of 1999, I contacted the Colorado Springs Utilities offices.  I managed to get in touch with an individual who stated he was an office manager for the utilities, and that he had some measure of control of personnel, resources and much knowledge of the existing power system in the Springs area.  He would not allow me to publish his name, his actual title or details of any of his "control" of the Colorado Springs Utilities. 

In our area, the utilities basically takes care of providing electricity, water, natural gas and sewage connections, for which we, as citizens pay the utilities for these services.  In other words, one department is basically responsible for a vast range of important services in the Colorado Springs area.   They have their own Information Services Department, apart from the Information Services department that handles the Police computers, as well as other city computer systems.  This means they are somewhat independant of the City Information Services Department, and as such therefore maintain computers that handle water, gas and electric.   I could not ascertain if sewage is handled through computers, though I doubt it is a metered function, since my own residence does not have an instrument in this regard.   I must therefore assume that all incoming, metered water is charged and then sewage is charged against this number as well.

In the course of the conversation with... let's call him John Smith (not his real name) I discovered that, while some minimal preparations have gone on, for Y2K, those preparations include some software upgrades and some hardware changes.  He would not elobrate on the depth of these changes and upgrades.   Neither would he go into details on the power grid, how the interconnections are made, or the possible ramafactions of computer glitches causing a catastrophic Nationwide power grid failure.  He did tell me he believed the system in our area is stable and will withstand outages across the country.  I'm afraid I differ with that opinion somewhat.  Here is why.

About four years ago - the exact date escapes me right now - myself and others were already "preaching" y2k preparedness.  We spoke, mostly of power grid problems, interspersed with information on items such as the Railroads, airlines and air traffic control, banking system glitches as well as things like credit card problems.  At that time, we didn't even think to mention things like IRS problems, Welfare checks, Social Security, Treasury Department problems, and so forth.     On, what I seem to recall as being a weekend (because I was doing Reserve Duty that weekend, and I think it was a Sunday) suddenly at about 12:30 PM Mountain time, the power went out. 

I remember thinking that it was strange we had lost commercial power on an Air Force Base.  I also remember going out to my vehicle and calling on the ham radio to find out how wide-spread the outage was.  I was astonished when a Ham from Denver told me that the power was out there as well.  A short time later, some of the radio stations in the region began coming back online.   One of them, KVOR, a local Spings station came on with the news that the power outage was effecting at least 4 or 5 other states.  Later than evening, I learned that at least ten states were affected, as well as some Canadian provences.

What I didn't know at the time, and have since learned from ABC news, was a software glitch in some newly installed software introduced this massive outage!  A couple of weeks ago, ABC news carried a y2k related story about software, embedded chip sets and similar news.  In that news article that now-infamous outage was mentioned.  As it happens, the ten states and Canadian provinces affected all --- get this now --- all happen to be the WESTERN US POWER GRID.  This can very easily be confirmed from the Senates OWN  y2k report put out a few months ago.  In that report  is a map showing the US power grid.  The map shows EXACTLY the states that lost power for several hours.  The day it went out, was very close to the opening date of the movie "Independance Day", a movie about alien invaders who attempt to exterminate the human race so they can strip the Earth of all of its natural resources.  We joked about the "aliens have landed". I remember making a crack about y2k, to which everyone said "huh?"  Obviously, I was far before my time on the jokes.

To this day, very few electric companies are considered "compliant". 

(See Senate Report http://www.senate.gov/~y2k/documents/report/index.html)  What does this mean?  It means that there are systems within many electricity production facilities that have not been corrected.   It means that, if we assume the worst case scenario, we will have a repeat performance of the Western US Power Grid Outage on or about 1 January 2000.  If any ONE system is glitched enough that a station goes out, a massive, cascade failure could result.  While many analysts have stated that things should be back to normal in no more than a day or so, if this occurs, the US Government, FEMA and several emergency relief agencies, including the Red Cross have stated unequovically that we should "prepare for a storm".

In Red Cross and FEMA terms, this means we should store food and water for a few days for our families.  We should have flashlights ready, our cars gassed up and basically prepare as if a hurricane were about to strike our regions.  This is, at the very least,  excellent and prudent advice.  What can it hurt for individuals to prepare for a blizzard, or hurricane?  What harm does it do the citizens of the United States to spend a little extra cash, some time and a tiny amount of effort to prepare --- JUST IN CASE?  Nothing.

However, as a computer professional, a 22 year (so-far) veteran of the United States Air Force, a government contractor, teacher, Presidental Communications Senior Radio Technician, husband, Father of Five and Grandfather of two - I suggest that those whom I've spoken to about this and have stated to me they neither have the inclination nor time to "bother" with "preparations" are absolute fools.  The WORST that can happen if you prepare, and nothing bad happens is, you have a few days supplies on hand.  You may find that you need them during some other surprise situation - ie Earthquake, volcanic erruption, blizzard, tornado, hurricane, tsunami or, heck, I'll say it here, asteroid strike on the world.

What happens if you do NOT prepare and the worst happens?  With all the hype about Y2K, there will still be those who do not prepare in any way, shape or form.  It will be those people, who, on 1 January 2000 as Midnight on the East Coast falls upon Times Square note that the power just went out, and will panic.   They will be the ones running to the all-night market to grab a few necessities, like food and water.  It will be this panic buying that causes small riots in large cities.  It will be the fact that the many caught unprepared ran out for food, buy out the supermarkets and gas stations (if indeed they can get gas pumped with no power) that cause OTHERS to panic. 

Large cities will be hit first and hardest.   Throughout the crisis, as the media will begin to call it in the first days, there will be little heroic stories of the elderly, children and the hospitialized being saved, volunteers manning the Ham Radios and passing messages around the clock.  There will be the National Guardsmen call out to prevent looting.  The Police departments will begin to be over worked.  Power, if not restored in a couple of days, will be demanded by citizens.  More small, but violent riots will break out, in regional areas, in particular cities.

Through out all of this, Third World Countries remain, largely unaffected - remember, they are not as dependant upon electricity as the United States.  Remember also, we are currently at war in two areas of the world as this is written.  If those two situations remain as they are today, there will be those in foreign counties that would LOVE to see the US down and out for a time.  There is the possibility that random terrorism will occur.  There is the possibility that NOT-SO-Random terrorism will occur! 

In other words, if the worst can happen, it will.   Don't think it will?  Don't think that the power will go off?  Think that the government can stop it, and fix everything?  Better think again.  One problem we, as American's seem to have in common with each other is that we do not believe "it" can happen to "us".  And yet, all around us, every day, people die in car accidents, freak accidents and by murder.  All around us, natural disasters occur on a regular basis.  Things happen in this world to each and every person, every day.  Those things can and will affect us!  Do I personally think Y2K will be the "end of the world as we know it"?  No, but, I do give credit where credit is due and the human race has an amazing, almost unlimited capacity to go above and beyond the call of duty.  I believe that leaders will step forth from the chaos and help to make the necessary course corrections to keep us on the right path (and I DO NOT believe they will be politicians).   I believe in the human race and its capacity to love and cherish life.  I also believe in the human race's capacity for ignorance, stupidity and hatred.  I believe in the ability of mankind to make war, and to do limitless destruction of the natural surroundings.  I believe that most, not all, human beings have the ability to take full advantage of his or her fellow human being and to take that which is not his or hers.

I believe that, what ever comes of Y2K, there will be a great sense of loss, and a great sense of accomplishment on all sides.  Personally, I want to be on the side of accomplishment. 

Therefore, I will be prepared for the worst - all the while, hoping for the best.
